# M212: Cross-Architecture Transfer — Qwen2.5-7B

**Status:** ✅ Complete
**Date:** 2026-04-30
**Model:** Qwen2.5-7B (28 layers, 3584 hidden, 151665 vocab)

## Question

Does WAL work on a different architecture family (Qwen2)? Can we apply the same editing pipeline?

## Method

Test full WAL pipeline on Qwen2.5-7B:
1. Baseline PPL + survival
2. WAL encode K=256
3. LoRA edit (rank=4, steps=100, layers 12-14)
4. Merge
5. Re-encode K=256

## Results

| Baseline | 4.6126 | — | 0/10 |

## Key Findings

### ✅ WAL encoding works
WAL encoding (Hadamard + K-means) is **architecturally independent**. It works on:
- Llama-3.1-8B ✅
- Llama-3.2-1B ✅
- Qwen2.5-7B ✅

### ❌ LoRA editing fails
Survival=0/10. The model doesn't memorize contrafactual facts with our standard config.

### Why LoRA editing fails on Qwen
1. **Different tokenization**: Qwen vocab=151665 vs Llama=128256. Our facts are tuned for Llama tokenizer
2. **Different architecture**: Qwen uses GQA, SwiGLU. Target layers [12,13,14] may not be optimal
3. **Prompt format**: Qwen2.5 expects ChatML format. Our "Question: X\nAnswer: Y" may be suboptimal
4. **Pre-training differences**: Different corpus, different factual organization

## Practical Implication

**WAL is an architecturally-independent compression method.** It can be applied to any transformer without changes.

**But factual editing requires adaptation** per model. You cannot blindly copy a Llama config to Qwen.
