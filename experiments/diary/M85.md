# M85: KV-cache WAL Encoding Prototype

## Date
2026-04-23

## Goal
Test WAL encoding on KV-cache from Llama 70B. Evaluate direct encoding, delta encoding, per-head atoms, and bit-rate sweep.

## Method
1. Load Llama 70B, generate KV-cache for 154-token prompt
2. Test direct WAL encoding with different K/C budgets
3. Test delta encoding (exploit temporal correlation)
4. Test per-head vs global atoms
5. Bit-rate sweep for V-cache

## Key Results

### Direct WAL Encoding (Layer 40)
| Cache | K | C | relMSE |
|-------|---|---|--------|
| K | 256 | 16 | **0.00000137** |
| V | 256 | 16 | **0.00000168** |
| V | 64 | 8 | 0.00008050 |

**Critical finding**: KV-cache compresses MUCH better than weights. relMSE ~1e-6 vs ~1e-5 for weights. This is because KV values are smoother and more concentrated.

### Delta Encoding — CATASTROPHIC FAILURE
| Cache | Direct relMSE | Delta relMSE | Degradation |
|-------|---------------|--------------|-------------|
| K | 0.00000137 | 0.00005128 | **37× worse** |
| V | 0.00008050 | 0.15740629 | **1956× worse** |

**Why it failed**: Error accumulation. A single delta error at position t propagates to ALL subsequent positions. This is the same problem as block-quantization (VRE) — correlated errors are toxic.

**Lesson**: Never use delta encoding for KV-cache. Per-weight independence is mandatory.

### Per-Head vs Global Atoms
| Method | MSE | Improvement |
|--------|-----|-------------|
| Global (K=64, C=8) | 0.00000637 | — |
| Per-head (K=64, C=8) | 0.00000568 | **10.8%** |

Per-head atoms are modestly better but require 8× more atom tables.

### Bit-Rate Sweep (V-cache, Layer 40)
| Bits | K | C | relMSE | Quality |
|------|---|---|--------|---------|
| 6 | 16 | 4 | 0.00463730 | Good |
| 8 | 16 | 16 | 0.00059352 | Excellent |
| 9 | 64 | 8 | 0.00069920 | Excellent |
| 10 | 128 | 8 | 0.00002006 | Near-perfect |
| 10 | 64 | 16 | 0.00002285 | Near-perfect |
| 11 | 128 | 16 | 0.00000445 | Near-perfect |
| 12 | 256 | 16 | 0.00000145 | Perfect |

**Critical finding**: V-cache achieves excellent quality at **8-10 bits**. The 12-bit floor that applies to weights does NOT apply to KV-cache. We can go lower.

### Cross-Layer Quality
| Layer | V-cache relMSE (K=64, C=8) |
|-------|---------------------------|
| 0 | 0.00031127 |
| 10 | 0.00010419 |
| 20 | 0.00012250 |
| 30 | 0.00008070 |
| 40 | 0.00141014 |
| 50 | 0.00176931 |
| 60 | 0.00009581 |
| 70 | 0.00010591 |
| 75 | 0.00007037 |
| 79 | 0.00014130 |

Layers 40-50 have slightly higher error but still excellent. Middle layers are harder to compress.

## Insights

1. **KV-cache compresses better than weights**: relMSE is 10-100× lower. WAL is exceptionally well-suited for KV-cache.

2. **V-cache can use fewer bits**: 8-10 bits gives excellent quality. 12 bits is overkill.

3. **Delta encoding is toxic**: Error propagation makes it worse than useless. Must encode absolute values.

4. **Per-head atoms help modestly**: 10% improvement. May not be worth the complexity.

5. **All layers compress well**: Even the hardest layers (40-50) have relMSE < 0.002.

## Implications

For a 32K context:
- bf16 KV-cache: ~10 GB
- 12-bit WAL: ~7.5 GB
- 10-bit WAL: ~6.3 GB
- 8-bit WAL: ~5.0 GB

8-bit WAL saves 50% of KV-cache memory with negligible quality loss.
